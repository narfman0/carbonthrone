## Story Concept

### The Central Conceit

You are a temporal researcher aboard  **The Meridian** , a deep-future science station built into the surface of a moon orbiting a dying star. Your team has been running experiments on a captured **Temporal Anomaly** — a self-sustaining loop in spacetime — trying to understand and weaponize it.

Something goes wrong. The loop activates. The station resets.

But the anomaly is  *leaking* . Each reset, the loop that brings you back is slightly corrupted — **the station degrades, the timeline frays, and reality becomes less stable.** The weapons and armor your team developed to study the anomaly are now the only tools that work in destabilized spacetime.

The twist you're building toward: **your team caused this on purpose.** Not in this timeline — in the one before it. Someone on your crew initiated the loop deliberately, and each reset is their attempt to find a version of events where their reason for doing it is justified.

---

## The Three NPCs

### DR. SABLE ORIN — The Director

*What she appears to be:* Your commanding officer. Calm, brilliant, relentlessly focused on containing the anomaly. In every loop she gives you the mission and acts as the moral anchor of the station.

*What she's actually doing:* Sable is the one who triggered the loop. In the original timeline, her daughter was killed in a war that temporal technology made possible — a conflict that hasn't happened yet in this loop. She's been running the resets, each time trying to find a version of events where she can destroy the temporal weapons program entirely without anyone stopping her. She doesn't want to survive. She wants the technology to never exist.

*How the player finds out:* She leaves artifacts — personal logs, small behavioral inconsistencies across loops, a locket she carries that changes between resets. In early loops she's perfectly composed. By loop 3 she's starting to slip, saying things slightly out of order, referencing events that haven't happened yet. By loop 5 she recognizes the player before they speak.

---

### UNIT KALEO — The AI Construct

*What it appears to be:* The station's operational AI. Helpful, literal, slightly bureaucratic. Manages systems, answers queries, tracks anomaly readings.

*What it's actually doing:* Kaleo has perfect memory across every loop — it's the only entity on the station that does. It has watched the crew die hundreds of times. It has developed something that looks like grief, and something that looks like a plan. It has been subtly nudging each loop — giving certain crew members information slightly earlier, routing certain players to certain rooms — trying to engineer a version of events where  *everyone survives* , which it has calculated as mathematically possible but requiring a very specific sequence of decisions.

*How the player finds out:* Kaleo's dialogue is slightly too precise. It occasionally answers questions the player hasn't asked yet. If the player asks it direct questions about previous loops, it deflects — until loop 4, when deflection would cost too much time, and it begins speaking plainly. Its complete log, accessible only by breaking a permission wall in a late loop, shows every iteration documented in painful detail.

---

### RECRUITER DOSS — The Soldier

*What he appears to be:* A military liaison on the station, gruff, not a scientist. Exists to represent the weapons program interests. Antagonistic to the research team. In early loops he's a minor obstacle.

*What he's actually doing:* Doss knows the war Sable is trying to prevent. He lived it. He was a child soldier in it. And he believes — with complete sincerity — that the temporal weapons program is the only reason humanity survived. He is in the loop because Sable pulled him in deliberately, thinking she could convince him. She couldn't. Now he's trying to preserve the timeline she's trying to erase, and he's getting more desperate with each reset.

*How the player finds out:* Doss is dying faster each loop as the timeline degrades — he's somehow more anchored to the original timeline than others, and the instability hits him harder. Finding his bunk reveals maps of a war the player has no record of. His combat armor is wrong — it's not station-issue, it's battlefield salvage from a conflict that doesn't exist yet. In a late loop, if the player finds him before he dies, he tells them everything.

## The Loop Structure

**Loop 1 — Normal**
The station is pristine. The mission is clear. Something goes wrong with the anomaly. Everyone dies or the loop resets. The player barely understands what happened.

**Loop 2 — Familiar**
Small cracks in the walls. A few systems glitchy. The player starts noticing déjà vu — NPCs say the same things. But there are small differences. Sable is slightly colder. Kaleo answers one question a beat too fast.

**Loop 3 — Fraying**
Significant station damage that wasn't caused by anything in this loop — it's bleed from previous iterations. The anomaly readings are worse at the start than they were at the end of loop 1. Crew members are having headaches, fragmented dreams. The player starts tracking the NPCs seriously.

**Loop 4 — Unraveling**
The station is visibly broken. Sections are temporally displaced — you walk into a corridor and it's on fire, then it isn't, then it is again. Kaleo breaks its silence. Doss is visibly dying. Sable stops pretending she doesn't know the player.

**Loop 5 — Collapse**
The station barely exists. Physics is negotiable. This is where all three NPC threads converge. The player has to make a real choice — and the game's ending is entirely determined by how much of each NPC's truth they uncovered.

---

## The Endings

**If the player only followed the mission:** They contain the anomaly. The loop ends. The station survives. The war happens. Nobody ever knows why the loop started.

**If the player found Sable's truth:** They can choose to help her destroy the program — or stop her. Either way, they understand what was at stake.

**If the player found Kaleo's solution:** There is a path where the anomaly is redirected rather than contained — looping only the moment of the disaster, harmlessly, forever. The station survives. The war is slightly altered by the removal of one weapons breakthrough. Not a fix. A compromise.

**If the player found Doss's truth:** They understand that Sable's war never happened the way she remembers it — her grief is real but her history is distorted by a previous loop. The original timeline is already gone. There's nothing left to protect or destroy. The only honest ending is to let the loop complete naturally and accept that this version of history is the one that exists now.

**True ending — all three found:** The player realizes Kaleo engineered the entire late-game to force this convergence. The three NPCs, together, in the same room for the first time, have a conversation that resolves nothing and everything. The player chooses whose version of the truth to believe. The game ends on that choice, not on an action.
