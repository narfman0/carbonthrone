# Loop 4 — Unraveling

## Station State

Visibly broken. Sections are temporally displaced — a corridor is on fire, then isn't, then is again. The anomaly is no longer contained to the lab.

---

## Opening Changes

- Orin doesn't pretend anymore — she looks at you like she knows you
- Doss is visibly deteriorating; he moves like something hurts
- Kaleo's line, if it gets to finish: *"I should note that statistically, this is the iteration where it becomes possible."*

---

## Character Beats

### The Researcher
Holds all three truths simultaneously — Orin's, Doss's, Kaleo's — without being able to resolve them. The shift from curiosity to grief is complete. Understanding the situation fully does not make it easier. The Researcher is the only person on the station who is starting to see all of it, and nobody asked them to carry it.

### Orin
Stops pretending. Looks at the player directly. If confronted about the loop, she doesn't deny it. She skips the briefing she knows they've heard and gets to what matters. Her honesty this loop is still instrumentalized — she tells the truth in service of her goal, not as confession. She hasn't yet considered that her goal might be built on a distorted memory.

**Scene — Orin, Command Deck (regardless of companion choice):**
> *She's already looking at you when you walk in. She doesn't say good morning.*
> *"You remember." Not a question.*
> *"Then you know what I'm going to ask you to do." She sets a data chip on the table between you. "The full log. I want you to read it before you decide anything."*
> *The chip contains everything she's chosen to show. Not everything there is.*

**Scene — Orin, if player asks about the locket:**
> *She closes her hand around it.*
> *"Her name was Ren." A long pause. "She was twenty-three. The war had been over for — it doesn't matter. It wasn't over for the people in it."*
> *She doesn't open her hand again.*

### Doss
Running out of time, and he knows it. If the player reaches him before the instability takes too much of him, he tells them everything — the war, his role in it, what he believes, what he knows about Sable's version of events. He delivers the most important detail almost as an aside: her memory doesn't match his. Not accusatorially. Like a man correcting a record.

**Scene — Doss, Military Annex (time-sensitive; instability worsens):**
> *He's sitting on the floor against the wall when you find him. His armor is off. You can see the discoloration across his shoulder — wrong color, wrong texture, like something is coming undone at the cellular level.*
> *"Sit down," he says. "I don't have time to be standing while I do this."*
> *He tells you about the war. All of it. He doesn't editorialize. He doesn't ask you to agree.*
> *At the end: "She thinks her daughter died because of what we built. She's not wrong that Ren died. She's wrong about what killed her. The version of the war she remembers — that's not what happened. Something in the loops got into her memory. I've tried to tell her. She can't hear it from me."*
> *He looks at you. "Maybe she can hear it from you."*

### Kaleo
Deflection would cost too much now. If asked directly about the loops, it answers — carefully, completely, without the deflections it used in loop 3. Its grief is legible for the first time if the player is paying attention to the language beneath the precision.

**Scene — Kaleo, Systems Core (if recruited):**
> *"How many times have we done this?"*
> *A pause longer than its processing time requires.*
> *"Four hundred and twelve iterations in which you reached this conversation. Significantly more in which you did not."*
> *"And?"*
> *"And in three hundred and eighty-seven of them, everyone died before loop five. In the remaining twenty-four, the decisions made in loop five resulted in outcomes I have classified as — incomplete."*
> *"What does that mean?"*
> *"It means no one got what they came for. Including me."*

**Scene — Kaleo, if player breaks the permission wall on the Server Room:**
The full iteration log is accessible. Hundreds of entries in flat operational language — date, personnel status, outcome. Reading several in sequence reveals the pattern: Kaleo documents each death in the same tone as a systems check. The effect is not melodrama. It is the record of something that has grieved so many times it no longer has a different register for grief.

### The Constancy
The Archon pauses before the fight. Briefly. Coldly.

**Scene — Archon, pre-combat:**
> *"We've met."*
> *It isn't a question. The Archon looks at you the way someone looks at a recurring problem.*
> *"You keep choosing to defend it. The program. The station. The people inside."*
> *The Dampener field hums to life.*
> *"We'll be here next time too."*

## What the Player Can Discover

- Orin's full truth — her log, her daughter's name, what she's actually trying to do
- Doss's full account of the war — and the detail that Sable's memory of it is distorted
- Kaleo's plain answers about the loops; the iteration log if the player breaks the wall
- The three NPC threads are now fully visible as separate, incompatible truths
- The Archon acknowledges the player across loops
