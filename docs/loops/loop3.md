# Loop 3 — Fraying

## Station State

Significant damage that wasn't caused by anything in this loop — bleed from previous iterations. The anomaly readings are worse at the start than they were at the end of loop 1. Crew members are having headaches, fragmented dreams.

---

## Opening Changes

- Orin is calm in a way that feels rehearsed
- Doss is already checking the door when you wake up
- Kaleo's cut-off line is different: *"I should note that I have run this calculation before — [drops]"*

---

## Character Beats

### The Researcher
Actively investigating now. Choosing paths that yield information over paths that yield safety. The growing weight of knowing something is wrong has a cost — NPCs start to feel less like colleagues and more like puzzles. The companion relationship deepens or strains depending on how hard the player pushes.

### Orin
The composure is cracking. She says things slightly out of order — references a decision the player hasn't made yet, corrects herself, moves on. The number of iterations is finally showing. If she's the companion: she begins partially opening her logs, framing it as trust. She is still deciding what to show and what to withhold. The locket has changed again. If asked about it, she pauses one beat too long before answering.

**Scene — Orin companion, Command Deck:**
> *You ask her about the logs she hasn't opened.*
> *She looks at the terminal. Then at you. "Some of it isn't — I'm not ready to show you all of it yet."*
> *She says "yet" like she means it. You don't know if she does.*

### Doss
With enough trust — which comes faster if he's the chosen companion — he references the war by name for the first time. Not explaining it. Mentioning it the way someone mentions a place they grew up, assuming you know where it is. He is also visibly worse — something in how he moves, how he holds his weight. If asked, he says it's nothing. It isn't nothing.

**Scene — Doss companion, Military Annex:**
> *You find the maps in his bunk. War maps. Places that don't exist in any database you can find.*
> *"Old habit," he says, from the doorway. He doesn't ask how you found them.*
> *He sits down on the edge of the bunk. Doesn't look at you. "You ever been in something you couldn't walk away from? Not because they stopped you. Because walking away meant it happened for nothing."*
> *He doesn't explain what "it" is. Not yet.*

### Kaleo
*"I would be more useful in the field."* — displayed once, on a terminal the player passes. This is Kaleo making its move. It has decided this iteration is worth accelerating. If the player accepts, Kaleo joins and begins operating openly — warnings about what's ahead, route suggestions, information that arrives just before it becomes useful. It does not explain how it knows. Not yet.

**Scene — Kaleo, first dialog after joining:**
> *"You're going to ask me how I knew about the guard patrol."*
> *You were.*
> *"I have access to the station's sensor grid." A beat. "That is the accurate answer."*
> *It's not the complete answer. It knows you know that.*

### The Constancy
A wounded Zealot can be left alive after an encounter. If the player does not finish them, they speak before dying — not to persuade, just to be heard:

> *"You think we're wrong. You'll understand why we came. Before the end."*

Their literature is now findable in multiple locations. Reading it reveals a coherent philosophy — not fanaticism, but a worked-out argument. The argument is not easy to dismiss.

## What the Player Can Discover

- Orin's personal logs (partial) — she controls access but is starting to slip
- Doss will reference the war obliquely; on companion route, the bunk scene unlocks
- Constancy literature: their doctrine, the concept of "the given shape of things"
- A wounded Zealot speaks before dying
- Kaleo can be recruited; its first dialog establishes that it knows more than it's saying

---

## Kaleo Recruitment

The terminal message appears once. The player chooses to accept or ignore it. If accepted, Kaleo joins the party. It does not explain itself.
