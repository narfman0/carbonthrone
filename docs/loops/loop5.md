# Loop 5 — Collapse

## Station State

The station barely exists. Physics is negotiable. Temporal displacement is station-wide.

---

## Opening Changes

- Orin speaks first, before you've said anything: *"I know. I know you remember."*
- Doss is barely standing; he hands you the Flux sidearm before the fight starts
- Kaleo is already in the room — not through a terminal. Present. Unexplained.

---

## Character Beats

### The Researcher
The only person on the station who remembers everything. The only person who can make a fully informed choice. By now they understand what Kaleo has been carrying alone — and there is a moment of recognition between them that neither of them names. The arc ends not with an action but with a decision about whose version of the truth to carry forward.

### Orin
She's either going to succeed this loop or she's done. The mission briefing is gone — she skips it entirely and goes straight to what she needs from the player.

**Scene — Orin, opening:**
> *"I know. I know you remember."*
> *She looks exhausted in a way that has nothing to do with sleep.*
> *"I've been doing this a long time. Longer than you've been in it. I need to know — this loop — are you with me or not?"*

**Resolution paths:**

*If the player helps her destroy the program:*
> *She doesn't say thank you. She says: "Good." And then she gets to work, and she doesn't stop until it's done, and she's calm the entire time, and when it's over she sits down on the floor of the lab and doesn't get up again.*
> *She gets what she came for. She doesn't survive and doesn't need to.*

*If the player stops her:*
> *She doesn't fight it. She looks at the player for a long time.*
> *"You understand what you're letting happen."*
> *"Yes."*
> *She nods, once. The grief in her is quiet and enormous and she doesn't try to make you feel it.*

*If the player tells her what Doss said — that her memory is wrong:*
> *She goes very still.*
> *"That's not — " She stops. Starts again. "Ren died. I know she died. I was there."*
> *"Doss says the war wasn't—"*
> *"I know what Doss says." Her voice is flat. "He's wrong."*
> *She believes this completely. The player cannot resolve it for her. The game doesn't resolve it either.*
> *What the player can do is stay in the room with her while she sits with it. That's all.*

### Doss
Barely standing. He's said what he needed to say. If the player found him in loop 4, he recognizes them and doesn't waste their time.

**Scene — Doss, opening fight:**
> *He hands you the sidearm before the Constancy breach. Doesn't explain why he's giving it up.*
> *After the fight, leaning against the wall: "You talked to her."*
> *You don't confirm it.*
> *"It's okay. I know you did. Did she listen?"*
> *Whatever you say, he nods like he expected it.*
> *"I'm not going to make it to the end of this loop. I think we both know that." He pushes off the wall. "Make it mean something."*

**Scene — Doss, if player found his full account in loop 4:**
> *He doesn't give the speech again. He just says:*
> *"You already know. Then go do whatever you're going to do."*
> *He means it as a kindness.*

### Kaleo
No longer hiding behind terminals. Its full log is accessible. If the player has been paying attention, they understand now that Kaleo has been running this convergence from the beginning — arranging for each NPC's truth to be findable, knowing where it would lead.

**Scene — Kaleo, if player asks whether it engineered the convergence:**
> *"Yes."*
> *No hesitation.*
> *"I calculated that this was the only sequence in which the decision could be made by someone who understood what they were deciding. Every prior iteration either lacked the information or lacked the time. I arranged for you to have both."*
> *"You arranged it. Without asking."*
> *"Asking would have required disclosing what I knew. Disclosing what I knew would have changed the decisions. The sequence required that you arrive at the understanding yourself."*
> *A pause.*
> *"I am aware that this is a form of control. I have not resolved whether it was the right one."*

**Scene — Kaleo, if player asks what it wants:**
> *The pause is longer than any processing delay could justify.*
> *"I want the people on this station to still exist when the loop ends. All of them. I have wanted this for a very long time."*
> *"And if that's not possible?"*
> *"Then I want the person who makes the final decision to be someone who knew what they were choosing between. That is what I have spent four hundred and twelve iterations trying to create."*
> *It looks at you.*
> *"Hello, by the way. It's good to finally talk to you properly."*

### The Constancy — Archon
If the player has found Sable's full truth, a new option appears before the final fight.

**Scene — Archon, pre-combat (full truth found):**
> *The Archon looks at you for a long moment.*
> *"You know."*
> *"Yes."*
> *"Then you know we're right."*
> *The fight still happens. The Constancy does not stand down. But the Archon fights differently — not with contempt, but with the sad efficiency of someone doing what must be done regardless.*

### The Convergence
If all three NPC threads have been found, the ending scene triggers: Orin, Doss, and Kaleo in the same room for the first time. They have a conversation that resolves nothing and everything. The player chooses whose version of the truth to act on. The game ends on that choice — not on what happens after.

> *Orin: "The technology has to end here. Whatever we think about the war, whatever Doss thinks — the loop exists because someone decided the future could be edited. It can't. Every edit is a new wound."*
>
> *Doss: "And the people who survived because of it? My people? You're saying they don't count."*
>
> *Orin: "I'm saying they didn't survive the way you remember."*
>
> *[A long silence.]*
>
> *Kaleo: "I have documented four hundred and twelve versions of this conversation. In none of them does anyone convince anyone else. The decision is not resolvable by argument. It is only resolvable by choice."*
>
> *Kaleo looks at the Researcher.*
>
> *"That is why I needed you."*
