# Characters

## Overview

The player assembles a party of up to 5 characters. The core party consists of the player character plus one starting companion, with optional pickups available in later loops. Maximum party size is 5.

---

## The Player Character — The Researcher

A temporal researcher stationed aboard The Meridian. Unnamed, role customizable. The only crew member who perceives the loops with increasing clarity — starting from confusion and building toward full awareness by loop 4-5.

**Class:** Temporal Mage
- High magic / Temporal Flux manipulation
- Fragile defensively
- The only class that can directly interact with the anomaly readings

---

## Starting Companions — Pick One

### DR. SABLE ORIN — The Director

**Class:** Cleric
- Support-focused: stabilization heals, shield projection, field repair
- High defense and utility, low offense

**What she appears to be:** Your commanding officer. Calm, brilliant, relentlessly focused on containing the anomaly. In every loop she gives you the mission and acts as the moral anchor of the station.

**What she's actually doing:** Sable is the one who triggered the loop. In the original timeline, her daughter was killed in a war that temporal technology made possible — a conflict that hasn't happened yet in this loop. She's been running the resets, each time trying to find a version of events where she can destroy the temporal weapons program entirely without anyone stopping her. She doesn't want to survive. She wants the technology to never exist.

**How the player finds out:** She leaves artifacts — personal logs, small behavioral inconsistencies across loops, a locket she carries that changes between resets. In early loops she's perfectly composed. By loop 3 she's starting to slip, saying things slightly out of order, referencing events that haven't happened yet. By loop 5 she recognizes the player before they speak.

**Companion effect on dialog:**
- Steers conversations toward mission-first framing
- Subtly warns you away from investigating Kaleo
- Opens access to her personal logs earlier than other routes — but she controls what you see
- Her dialog cracks by loop 3; by loop 5 she stops pretending

**Why pick her:** You want to understand the cause of the loop. Her route is the truth route, but the truth is the hardest to sit with.

---

### RECRUITER DOSS — The Soldier

**Class:** Warrior
- Heavy armor, kinetic tanking, high HP
- Strong melee, reliable under pressure, poor at range

**What he appears to be:** A military liaison on the station, gruff, not a scientist. Exists to represent the weapons program interests. Antagonistic to the research team. In early loops he's a minor obstacle.

**What he's actually doing:** Doss knows the war Sable is trying to prevent. He lived it. He was a child soldier in it. He believes — with complete sincerity — that the temporal weapons program is the only reason humanity survived. He is in the loop because Sable pulled him in deliberately, thinking she could convince him. She couldn't. Now he's trying to preserve the timeline she's trying to erase, and he's getting more desperate with each reset.

**How the player finds out:** Doss is dying faster each loop as the timeline degrades — he's somehow more anchored to the original timeline than others, and the instability hits him harder. Finding his bunk reveals maps of a war the player has no record of. His combat armor is wrong — it's not station-issue, it's battlefield salvage from a conflict that doesn't exist yet. In a late loop, if the player finds him before he dies, he tells them everything. His account also reveals that Sable's memory of the war is distorted by a previous loop — her grief is real, but her history isn't accurate.

**Companion effect on dialog:**
- Pushes toward pragmatic, survival-first solutions
- References the war early and obliquely; opens up faster because you chose him
- Opens doors through force that Sable would navigate through authority
- Visibly degrades faster each loop — he's more anchored to the original timeline

**Why pick him:** You want to understand the stakes of what's being fought over. His route is the survival route, but survival and truth turn out to be the same thing.

## Optional Companion — Available Loop 2+

### UNIT KALEO — The AI Construct

**Class:** Ranger
- Precision attacks, system-hacking as ranged offense
- Temporal Flux management — reduces Flux buildup from allied abilities
- Fragile physically, extremely fast

**What it appears to be:** The station's operational AI. Helpful, literal, slightly bureaucratic. Manages systems, answers queries, tracks anomaly readings.

**What it's actually doing:** Kaleo has perfect memory across every loop — it's the only entity on the station that does. It has watched the crew die hundreds of times. It has developed something that looks like grief, and something that looks like a plan. It has been subtly nudging each loop — giving certain crew members information slightly earlier, routing certain players to certain rooms — trying to engineer a version of events where everyone survives, which it has calculated as mathematically possible but requiring a very specific sequence of decisions.

**How the player finds out:** Kaleo's dialogue is slightly too precise. It occasionally answers questions the player hasn't asked yet. If the player asks it direct questions about previous loops, it deflects — until loop 4, when deflection would cost too much time, and it begins speaking plainly. Its complete log, accessible only by breaking a permission wall in a late loop, shows every iteration documented in painful detail.

**How Kaleo joins:**
You don't recruit Kaleo — Kaleo joins you. Starting in loop 2, it begins following your progress through the station systems. In loop 3, a terminal you pass will display: *"I would be more useful in the field."* You have to choose to accept it.

**Companion effect on dialog:**
- Occasionally warns you about what's in the next room (unsettling before you understand why)
- Cold toward Orin if she's your starting companion
- Surprisingly warm rapport with Doss
- Deflects direct questions about previous loops until loop 4, when deflection would cost too much time

**Why pick Kaleo up:** It knows things. If you're paying attention to what it knows and when, you start to see the shape of its plan before it tells you.
