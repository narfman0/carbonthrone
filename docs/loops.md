# Loop Structure

Each loop resets the station, but the anomaly is leaking — station state degrades, NPCs accumulate behavioral tells, and the player retains memory. By loop 5, reality is negotiable.

---

## Loop 1 — Normal

### Station State
Pristine. Systems nominal. The mission is clear.

### Opening — Room 7-C

You come to on the floor. Ears ringing. Emergency lighting. The bulkhead door is being cut through from the outside — temporal-displaced hostiles, their forms flickering, half-present.

Orin is already up, shield projector running, calling positions in clipped commands. Doss has his back to a pillar, kinetic rifle up, swearing under his breath. Kaleo's voice comes through the room terminal — "I am calculating optimal engagement zones" — calm as always.

This fight is the tutorial. Each character demonstrates their role:
- **Orin** drops a stabilization field (healing / shield support)
- **Doss** tanks a charge and punts an enemy through a wall (heavy kinetic)
- **Kaleo** calls a flanking route a half-second before you'd think to look for one (precision / systems)

### The Split

Hostiles repelled. The anomaly readings spike. The station shudders. A three-way split in the corridor — each NPC's section of the station is being sealed by emergency bulkheads. Someone hands you a Flux sidearm. You have maybe thirty seconds.

Each character says one thing before the doors seal:

> *Orin, quietly:* "Stay with me. I know what has to be done."

> *Doss, direct:* "You want to survive this? Stick with me. I've been in worse."

> *Kaleo, from the terminal:* "I should note that statistically, your survival odds are highest if — [connection drops as their section seals]"

You walk toward one door. It opens. The others seal. The NPCs on the other side don't wave or protest — they just go. Efficient. Like they expected it.

### What the Player Can Discover
- Basic station layout and mission briefing
- The anomaly is unstable and the containment plan is underway
- Nothing feels wrong yet

### How it Ends
Something goes wrong with the anomaly. Everyone dies, or the loop resets. The player barely understands what happened.

## Loop 2 — Familiar

### Station State
Small cracks in the walls. A few systems glitchy. Nothing that couldn't be explained away.

### Opening Changes
- Orin is slightly colder than she was
- Kaleo answers one question a beat too fast
- Doss glances at the door a moment before the cutting starts

### What the Player Can Discover
- NPCs say nearly the same things — but not exactly
- Kaleo begins following the player's progress through station systems
- Doss's armor is wrong on close inspection — battlefield salvage, not station-issue
- Orin's locket is different than it was in loop 1

### Kaleo Availability
Starting this loop, a terminal near the player's path will occasionally display Kaleo's status. It is watching. It has not said anything about it yet.

---

## Loop 3 — Fraying

### Station State
Significant damage that wasn't caused by anything in this loop — bleed from previous iterations. The anomaly readings are worse at the start than they were at the end of loop 1. Crew members are having headaches, fragmented dreams.

### Opening Changes
- Orin is calm in a way that feels rehearsed
- Doss is already checking the door when you wake up
- Kaleo's cut-off line is different: *"I should note that I have run this calculation before — [drops]"*

### What the Player Can Discover
- Orin's personal logs (partial) if she is the companion — she controls access but is starting to slip
- Doss will reference the war obliquely if pressed; he trusts you more on his companion route
- A terminal near the player's path displays: *"I would be more useful in the field."* — Kaleo can be recruited this loop

### Kaleo Recruitment
The terminal message appears once. The player chooses to accept or ignore it. If accepted, Kaleo joins the party. It does not explain itself.

---

## Loop 4 — Unraveling

### Station State
Visibly broken. Sections are temporally displaced — a corridor is on fire, then isn't, then is again. The anomaly is no longer contained to the lab.

### Opening Changes
- Orin doesn't pretend anymore — she looks at you like she knows you
- Doss is visibly deteriorating; he moves like something hurts
- Kaleo's line, if it gets to finish: *"I should note that statistically, this is the iteration where it becomes possible."*

### What the Player Can Discover
- Kaleo breaks its silence — deflection would cost too much time now. It begins speaking plainly about the loops if asked directly
- Doss, found early enough, tells the player about the war — all of it
- Orin's full log is accessible if the player breaks the permission wall in her terminal
- The three NPC threads are visible as separate, incompatible truths

---

## Loop 5 — Collapse

### Station State
The station barely exists. Physics is negotiable. Temporal displacement is station-wide.

### Opening Changes
- Orin speaks first, before you've said anything: *"I know. I know you remember."*
- Doss is barely standing; he hands you the Flux sidearm before the fight starts
- Kaleo is already in the room — not through a terminal. Present. Unexplained.

### What the Player Can Discover
- All three NPC threads converge
- Kaleo's complete log is accessible — every iteration, documented
- Doss's full account of the war, and the detail that Sable's memory of it is distorted by a previous loop
- Sable's reason, in her own words

### The Ending
Determined entirely by what the player found across all five loops. See `narrative.md` for ending conditions.
